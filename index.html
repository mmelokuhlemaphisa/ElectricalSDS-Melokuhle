Safe Data Sheet (SDS) – OSHA Electrical Safety Standards

Identification

  • Product: Electrical installation and tools
  • Use: Construction and management
  • Application Standards: OSHA 1910.137(b)(2), 1926.416

OSHA Standards Overview

1910.137(b)(2): PPE

  1. Rubber gloves
  2. Face shields
  3. Safety shoes or boots
  4. Insulating sleeves
  5. Flame-resistant clothing

1926.431: Maintenance

  1. Explosion-proofing
  2. Dust-proofing
  3. General upkeep
  4. Immediate repair when hazards are found

1926.416: Circuit Safety

  1. Label all circuits clearly
  2. Inform workers before entering live areas
  3. Inspect circuits regularly

Hazard Identification

  • Electric Shock
  • Fire Hazard
  • Wet Conditions
  • Tripping Hazards
  • Jewelry Conductivity
  • Unsafe Lab Equipment (e.g. Electrophoresis Unit)

General Electrical Safety Tips

  • Make a Plan
  • Wear the Right Gear
  • Use the Buddy System
  • Apply Lock Out/Tag Out (LOTO)
  • Avoid Wet Areas
  • Use GFCI Protection
  • Stay Away from Overhead Power Lines
  • Inspect Power Cords and Outlets for Damage

Lock Out / Tag Out (LOTO) Steps

  1. Inspect the System
  2. Shut Down the Circuit
  3. Isolate the Equipment
  4. Check for Stored Energy
  5. Verify Everything is Safe

Safe Use Of Electrophoresis Equipment

  • Chemical hazards
  • Electrical shock (100V can be lethal)
  • UV radiation
  • PPE requirements:
    1. Long-sleeved lab coat
    2. Nitrile gloves
    3. Safety goggles

Emergency Response

  • CALL 911 for unconscious person
  • Disconnect power before rescue
  • Use non-conductive tools
